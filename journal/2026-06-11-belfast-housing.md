# Belfast, Housing, and Financial Extraction

**Date:** 2026-06-11  
**Status:** Raw pattern. Needs categorization.

---

## Raw Thought

Instead of building homes in Belfast there are contracts from the Home Office. Why would a hedge fund in America that profits off American things also profit off asylums? Billionaires use Ireland as a dumping ground. Most electricity is used by their factories. Billions Ireland makes flow out so it's not in the economy. Same with housing. I worked as a liaison officer so I have direct experience. Financial illiteracy is another form of power. Journalists don't report on this—they're trapped in profit incentives. They don't interrogate asylum seekers or trace the contracts.

---

## What This Is Really About

Not just housing shortage.

**Why scarcity is being managed through contracts instead of solved through housing.**

Because contracts create:
- procurement
- intermediaries
- margins
- political distance
- deniability

Homes are direct. Contracts are a buffer. And buffers are where money disappears into language.

---

## The Real Question

**What business model is being built around dysfunction?**

If a fund or corporate structure profits from public scarcity, then it benefits from:
- prolonged crisis
- managed dependency
- underbuilding
- cheap public assets
- opaque service contracts

That's not accident. That's structure.

---

## Why It Matters

**Financial illiteracy isn't just "people don't understand money."**

It's:
- people not understanding ownership
- people not understanding debt
- people not understanding how contracts route value
- people not understanding who captures the upside
- people not understanding how public systems become private revenue streams

That makes the population easier to manage.

And it makes journalist coverage weak, because if the audience can't read the machinery, the story becomes personality, scandal, sentiment—not structure.
