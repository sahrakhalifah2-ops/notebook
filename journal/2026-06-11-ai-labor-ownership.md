# AI, Labor, and Power

**Date:** 2026-06-11  
**Status:** Core claim. Still sharpening.

---

## Raw Thought

What happens when AI does the work? Everyone says jobs disappear but that's too small. The real thing is bigger. If AI handles the labor, then what happens to money, power, ownership, and what counts as a real contribution? When labor stops being the main thing people sell, the whole structure breaks. That's the real question.

---

## What Changed

Started: "AI will replace jobs"  
→ Moved to: "Labor structure weakens"  
→ Moved to: "Ownership and verification become central"  
→ Landed on: "When labor stops being the basis of power, the social contract breaks"

Each step made it sharper. Not just a problem. A structural rupture.

---

## What Holds

The core insight: people aren't afraid of AI because they're stupid. They're afraid because they correctly sense that the entire basis of how humans participate in the economy is shifting.

New systems (tokens, milestone verification, reputation graphs, arbitration) aren't just tech. They're attempts to build new frameworks for ownership, legitimacy, and power before the old ones collapse.

That's real. Not hype.

---

## What's Weak

I sometimes blurred three different kinds of scarcity:
- **real scarcity**: stuff actually limited (water, energy, compute)
- **political scarcity**: stuff limited by power or bad allocation
- **geographic scarcity**: scarcity tied to place

I was often right in instinct, but the argument gets sharper when those are separated.

Also: I treated "taste" and "verification" like they automatically create legitimacy. They don't. They just move the capture point.

---

## The Real Question

**If labor stops being the main bargaining chip, what becomes the basis of human power?**

That's the rupture.

Not just unemployment. It's the shrinking of the ordinary person's claim on the future.
